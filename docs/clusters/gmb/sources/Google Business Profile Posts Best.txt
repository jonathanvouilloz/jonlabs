Standard Operating Procedure for Scheduling Google Business Profile Posts
 
Written by Claudia Tomina — Google Business Profile Product Expert
 
Google Business Profile (GBP) posts are a critical bottom-of-funnel marketing tool that appear directly in Google Search and Maps. Unlike social media, GBP is designed for conversion—users are actively searching for your business and ready to take action. This SOP ensures all posts comply with Google’s policies, maximize visibility, and drive measurable results through strategic CTAs and landing pages.
 
Key Success Metrics
Compliance with Google’s strict policies (no phone numbers in text or media)
Clear, action-oriented CTAs with properly tagged landing pages (UTM parameters)
Bottom-of-funnel messaging that answers last-mile questions (price, timing, availability)
Strategic posting cadence to keep high-value offers prominently displayed

Why GBP Posts ≠ Social Media
 
Google may automatically pull posts from your social channels for eligible businesses. If you duplicate social captions on GBP, you risk:
Redundant content: Users see the same post twice, reducing effectiveness
Missed conversion opportunities: Social copy is awareness-focused, not action-focused
Poor user experience: Generic messaging doesn’t answer last-mile questions
The Bottom-of-Funnel Mindset
When someone finds your GBP, they’re actively searching for your business—they’re ready to convert. Your posts should:
Answer last-mile questions: price, availability, timing, location, redemption steps
Include a clear CTA that drives immediate action (Book, Order, Shop, Reserve)
Link to specific landing pages where users can complete the action

Post Creation Workflow
 
Step 1: Choose the Right Post Type
Updates: General announcements, new products/services, company news
Offers: Limited-time promotions with specific discount/value
Events: Time-specific happenings (performances, classes, tastings)
Step 2: Prepare Your Content
Copy: 100-300 characters, lead with value, include timing and redemption
Media: One high-quality image or video (no phone numbers or excessive text)
CTA Button: Book, Order, Shop, Learn more, Sign up, or Call
Landing Page: Direct link where users complete the action (with UTM parameters)
Available CTA Button Options
CTA Options Available for Posts
 
Step 3: Create the Post in GBP
Open your Business Profile → Posts → Add post
Select post type (Update, Offer, or Event)
Add copy (value proposition in first 100-150 characters)
Upload one image or video
Complete required fields (title, dates for Offers/Events)
Add CTA button and landing page URL (include UTM parameters)
Preview on mobile and desktop if possible
Step 4: Add UTM Parameters for Tracking
 
Always tag your landing page URLs with UTM parameters to track performance:
source=google
medium=organic
campaign=gbp_post
Use Google’s UTM Builder: https://ga-dev-tools.google/ga4/campaign-url-builder/
 
Step 5: Schedule Strategically
Events: Publish early (1-2 weeks ahead) to give Google time to index
Offers: Align with peak demand periods (weekends, holidays)
Cadence: Avoid posting too frequently—keep high-value offers front and center

 
Post Scheduling Option
 
Step 6: Copy to Multiple Locations (If Applicable)
For businesses with multiple locations, Google offers a time-saving feature to copy the same post across up to 20 locations at once.
How It Works
After completing your post: When you click ‘Post,’ Google will prompt you with an option to copy the update to other profiles you manage
Select locations: Choose which locations should receive the same post (maximum 20 locations)
Confirm or skip: You can either apply to selected locations or skip to post only to the current location
Post is scheduled: Once confirmed, the post is scheduled for all selected locations simultaneously

 
Copy Post to Other Locations
 
When to Use Multi-Location Posting
Recommended for: Company-wide announcements, brand promotions, seasonal campaigns, or events happening at all locations
Avoid for: Location-specific offers, events unique to one store, or content mentioning specific addresses/landmarks
Pro tip: This feature is ideal for franchise businesses or multi-location brands maintaining consistent messaging across all storefronts
Offer Posts: Detailed Checklist
Use Offer posts for limited-time promotions and deals. These are the strongest conversion drivers when complete.
Define the offer: discount or value, applicable recipients, and exclusions
Create post → Offer type
Add a clear title (e.g., ‘Buy 1 Get 1 Free Burgers’)
Set precise start and end dates
Add coupon code if applicable
Include terms and conditions: exclusions, limits, store eligibility
Upload one high-quality photo or video (exclude phone numbers)
Write 100–300 characters describing value, scarcity, and redemption
Add ‘View offer’ link to landing page with redemption details
Include UTM parameters in URL (source=google, medium=organic, campaign=gbp_post)
Preview on mobile and desktop after publishing
Offer Copy Framework
Value + Scarcity + Redemption Path
Example: “Save 20% on online orders this week. Use code SAVE20 at checkout. Tap View offer for details.”
 
Event Posts: Getting Featured in Google Maps
Google Maps is integrating more experiences for the user. With Gemini integrated into Maps, we’re now seeing live events, classes, and activities surface directly inside the app.
 
Event Post Requirements
Title: Clear, descriptive (e.g., ‘Live Jazz Night’ not ‘Event’)
Exact date and time: No vague phrasing like ‘this weekend’
High-quality media: Show the experience, not just text
CTA: Book, Sign up, Learn more, or Reserve
Landing page: Event details, ticket link, or registration form
How to Get Events Featured
Publish early: 1-2 weeks ahead to give Google time to index
Complete metadata: Title, exact dates/times, location
Landing page signals: Include structured event details on your website
Consistent naming: Use same event name across GBP, website, ticketing partners
Avoid Ambiguous Phrasing
Google won’t show events with unclear timing:
 

Event Copy Framework
Hook + When/Where + CTA
Example: “Live jazz returns Friday 8-11pm. Reserve your table now.”

Optimization Tips for Maximum Visibility
 
Posting Cadence
Don’t over-post: Posting too frequently pushes current offers down
Keep high-value content front and center: Prioritize your best offers
For recurring events: Set start/end dates for the entire month instead of reposting weekly
Media Best Practices
Use high-quality, on-brand hero visuals designed to convert
Show the product or experience users will get
No phone numbers or excessive text overlay
One image or video per post (more is not better)
Landing Page Strategy
Every CTA should link to a specific, relevant landing page
Landing pages must match the offer/event details
Include clear redemption steps on the landing page
Linking to social media pages is allowed and encouraged
Always add UTM parameters for tracking
